opulation & sampling: Define eligible athletes (e.g., competitive 13,17 y/o). Sample using stratified random sampling (strata: sex, age-group, sport) to ensure balance, then obtain consent/assent. Baseline (O1): collect RESTQ, PRS, POMS, sleep diary/actigraphy, HRV (morning RMSSD), CMJ, training exposure log, and record previous injuries. Randomization (R): use computer RNG with seed; block randomization stratified by key covariates (sex, sport) to keep groups balanced. Intervention (X): deliver a manualised psychological coaching program (e.g., 8 weekly 45-min sessions: stress management, imagery, relaxation, sleep hygiene, return-to-play coping). Track attendance, homework, and fidelity checklist. Active control: match contact time with neutral content (e.g., general sport nutrition/skill workshops) to control for attention. Post-intervention (O2): repeat recovery and performance measures within 1,2 weeks after the last session. Follow-up (O3): continuous injury surveillance for 12 months (injury incidence per 1,000 exposure hours), monthly PRS, quarterly CMJ or HRV checks. Blinding: outcome adjudicators for injuries blinded to group allocation; participants cannot be blinded to receiving coaching. Data logging: keep sampling frame, RNG seed, recruitment log, fidelity scores, and exposure hours.
